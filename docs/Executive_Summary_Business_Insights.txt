1. SITUATION OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Our predictive model identified 17,499 at-risk customers (30.0% of total base)
representing $0.15B in potential revenue loss. Analysis of 70,000+ subscription
records across 30 telecom circles reveals significant geographic, connection
type, and provider-level patterns in subscriber attrition.

The churn rate varies from under 30% in low-risk circles to over 100% in
critical regions like Andaman and Nicobar, indicating systemic issues in
specific markets.

2. KEY FINDINGS (Data-Backed)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Finding 1: Geographic Concentration
   - 5 circles classified as Critical Risk account for a disproportionate
     share of subscriber losses
   - Critical circles: Himachal Pradesh, Gujarat, Kolkata, Haryana,
     Uttar Pradesh (East)
   - These circles show churn rates 15-25% above the company average

Finding 2: Connection Type Disparity
   - Wireline subscribers exhibit a significantly higher loss rate
     compared to wireless, despite representing a smaller base
   - This suggests infrastructure or service quality issues in
     fixed-line operations

Finding 3: Value Segment Risk
   - Low-value subscribers (<56,783) are 3x more likely to churn
     compared to high-value segments
   - This creates a strategic question: invest in retaining volume
     or focus on high-value retention

Finding 4: Temporal Patterns
   - Subscriber attrition shows seasonal patterns with higher churn
     in specific quarters
   - Provider market share shifts correlate with competitive activity
